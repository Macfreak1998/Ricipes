# Cocktails and Drinks in James Bond Novels - Research Report

## Overview

This research documents all cocktails and drinks mentioned in Ian Fleming's original James Bond novels and short stories. Bond consumes approximately **317 drinks** across Fleming's works, with an average weekly intake of 92 units of alcohol.

### Key Statistics
- **Dry Martini**: Mentioned 58 times
- **Gin Martinis**: 16
- **Vodka Martinis**: 19
- **Favorite Bourbon**: Old Grandad
- **Favorite Champagne**: Taittinger (also Dom Pérignon, Bollinger, Krug)
- **Beer**: Miller High Life

### Famous Quote
The immortal line "shaken and not stirred" first appeared in print in **Diamonds Are Forever** (1956).

## Novels in Chronological Order

### 1. Casino Royale (1953)

**Cocktails:**
- **The Vesper Martini** (Bond's invention, named after Vesper Lynd)
  - Recipe: "Three measures of Gordon's, one of vodka, half a measure of Kina Lillet. Shake it very well until it's ice-cold, then add a large thin slice of lemon peel. Got it?"
  - Served in a deep champagne goblet
  - Note: Bond specified grain-based vodka over potato vodka if possible
- **Americano** (Bond's first drink in the novels)
  - Campari, sweet Vermouth, and soda water (must be Perrier according to Bond)

**Other Drinks:**
- Taittinger Blanc de Blanc Brut 1943 (ordered as room service)
- Veuve Clicquot Ponsardin Brut
- Quarter-bottles of champagne
- Dry Martinis
- Whisky on the rocks

### 2. Live and Let Die (1954)

**Cocktails:**
- **Old Fashioned** (first appearance in Bond novels)
  - Specifically requested "Old Grandad" bourbon
  - Ordered on the Silver Phantom train with Solitaire
- **Medium Dry Martini**
  - Made with House of Lords gin and Martini & Rossi vermouth
  - Ordered at the King Cole Bar with Felix Leiter

**Other Drinks:**
- Miniature bottles of dry martini
- Blue Mountain coffee
- Old Grandad bourbon

### 3. Moonraker (1955)

## Notable Drink Specifications

### The Vesper Martini Recipe (Casino Royale)
- 3 measures Gordon's gin
- 1 measure vodka (preferably grain-based)
- 1/2 measure Kina Lillet
- Shake very well until ice-cold
- Add large thin slice of lemon peel
- Serve in deep champagne goblet

**Note:** Kina Lillet ceased production in 1986. Modern versions use Lillet Blanc or Cocchi Americano as substitutes.

### The Americano (Casino Royale)
- Campari
- Sweet Vermouth
- Soda water (must be Perrier, according to Bond)

### The Old Fashioned (Live and Let Die)
- Old Grandad bourbon (specified)
- Traditional Old Fashioned preparation

### The Stinger (Diamonds Are Forever, Thunderball)
- White Crème de Menthe (specified)
- Brandy
- Served with coffee

### The Negroni (For Your Eyes Only - "Risico")
- Gordon's gin (specified)
- Traditional Negroni preparation

### Gin and Tonic (Dr. No)
- Gin
- Tonic
- Juice of an entire lime
