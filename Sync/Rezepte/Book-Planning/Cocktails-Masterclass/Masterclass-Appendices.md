## Appendix A: Ingredient Deep Dive

### Spirits by Category

**Gin**
- London Dry: Juniper-forward, classic (Beefeater, Tanqueray, Bombay)
- Plymouth: Slightly sweeter, less juniper
- Old Tom: Sweeter, botanical, historical style
- Navy Strength: 57% ABV, more intense
- Contemporary/New Western: Various botanical profiles

**Vodka**
- Russian/Polish: Traditional, wheat or rye base
- French: Grape base, softer
- American: Various bases, neutral

**Rum**
- White/Silver: Light, fresh, Cuban style
- Gold: Aged briefly, caramel color sometimes added
- Dark: Aged longer, molasses-forward
- Jamaican: Funky, estery (pot still)
- Demerara: Rich, molasses, Guyanese
- Agricole: Grassy, vegetal (sugarcane juice base)
- Navy Strength: High proof (54.5%+)

**Whiskey**
- Bourbon: Corn-based, sweet, American oak
- Rye: Rye-based, spicier, American
- Scotch (blended): Mix of grain and malt, accessible
- Scotch (single malt): Regional variations, complex
- Irish: Triple distilled typically, smooth
- Japanese: Scotch-influenced, refined

**Tequila**
- Blanco: Unaged, pure agave character
- Reposado: 2-12 months aged, balanced
- Añejo: 1-3 years aged, oak influence
- Mezcal: Smoky, artisanal, various agave species

**Brandy**
- Cognac: French grape brandy, specific region
- Armagnac: French, single distilled, rustic
- Pisco: Peruvian/Chilean grape brandy
- Calvados: Apple brandy, French
- American Brandy: Various styles

### Essential Liqueurs

**Orange Liqueurs**
- Cointreau: Clear, balanced sweet-bitter
- Grand Marnier: Cognac base, richer
- Curaçao: Orange peel, various brands
- Triple Sec: Generic category, quality varies

**Herbal/Bitter**
- Campari: Bitter, essential for Negronis
- Aperol: Lighter, sweeter than Campari
- Chartreuse (Green): 130 herbs, 55% ABV, intense
- Chartreuse (Yellow): Sweeter, 40% ABV
- Bénédictine: Herbal, honey notes

**Nut/Fruit**
- Amaretto: Almond flavor
- Maraschino: Cherry pit, dry
- Chambord: Black raspberry
- Frangelico: Hazelnut
- Créme de Cassis: Black currant
- Créme de Mure: Blackberry

**Coffee/Cream**
- Kahlúa: Coffee, sweet
- Mr Black: Coffee, less sweet
- Baileys: Irish cream

### Vermouth and Fortified Wine

**Sweet Vermouth**
- Carpano Antica Formula: Rich, vanilla
- Cocchi Vermouth di Torino: Complex, balanced
- Dolin Rouge: Lighter, French style
- Punt e Mes: Bitter-sweet, almost amaro-like

**Dry Vermouth**
- Dolin Dry: Standard, reliable
- Noilly Prat: Oxidative style
- Martini Extra Dry: Accessible

**Other Fortified**
- Sherry (Fino): Dry, saline
- Sherry (Amontillado): Nutty, medium
- Sherry (Oloroso): Rich, oxidized
- Lillet Blanc: Wine-based aperitif
- Cocchi Americano: Quinquina style

### Bitters

**Aromatic**
- Angostura: The standard, baking spices
- Peychaud's: Floral, anise, New Orleans

**Citrus**
- Orange bitters: Various brands, essential
- Grapefruit bitters: Specialty

**Specialty**
- Chocolate bitters
- Celery bitters
- Mole bitters

## Appendix B: Equipment Guide

### Essential (start here)

1. **Jigger** (Japanese style, with internal markings)
2. **Boston shaker** (two-tin)
3. **Bar spoon** (long, twisted handle)
4. **Hawthorne strainer**
5. **Citrus juicer** (handheld press)
6. **Y-peeler** (for citrus twists)
7. **Muddler** (flat-bottomed, unvarnished)
8. **Knife and cutting board**

### Recommended (add as you progress)

9. **Fine-mesh strainer** (for double straining)
10. **Mixing glass** (weighted, with spout)
11. **Julep strainer**
12. **Lewis bag and mallet** (for crushed ice)
13. **Channel knife** (for citrus spirals)
14. **Speed pourers** (for bottles)
15. **Ice molds** (2-inch cubes)

### Advanced (for enthusiasts)

16. **Immersion circulator** (sous vide)
17. **iSi whip** (carbonation, infusions)
18. **Smoking gun**
19. **Scales** (for precision)
20. **Vacuum sealer**

### Glassware Priority

1. **Coupes** (6 oz) - most shaken drinks
2. **Rocks glasses** (10-12 oz) - Old Fashioneds, Negronis
3. **Highball glasses** (10-12 oz) - built drinks
4. **Collins glasses** (12-14 oz) - Collinses, tall drinks
5. **Champagne flutes** - sparkling drinks
6. **Nick and Nora** (5-6 oz) - small stirred drinks

## Appendix C: Troubleshooting Common Problems

### Drink is too sweet
- Add acid (citrus juice, even a few drops)
- Add bitters
- Dilute slightly
- Reduce sweetener next time

### Drink is too sour
- Add sweetener (bar spoon of syrup)
- Dilute more
- Check citrus freshness

### Drink is too bitter
- Add sweetness
- Serve colder
- Dilute more

### Drink is too strong/hot
- Dilute more (stir/shake longer)
- Add more mixer
- Serve colder

### Drink is too weak/watery
- Reduce dilution next time
- Use colder/harder ice
- Increase spirit proportion

### Drink is cloudy (should be clear)
- Don't shake (stir)
- Strain better
- Clean glassware

### Foam won't form (egg drinks)
- Dry shake longer
- Use fresher eggs
- Shake harder

### Carbonation is flat
- Don't shake carbonated mixers
- Use cold mixer
- Add carbonation last, stir minimally

## Appendix D: Building Your Home Bar

### The 8-Bottle Bar

Start here. These bottles enable 50+ classic cocktails:

1. **Gin** (London Dry - Beefeater or Tanqueray)
2. **Vodka** (any quality brand)
3. **Bourbon** (Buffalo Trace or Four Roses)
4. **Rum** (white - Plantation 3 Stars or Havana Club)
5. **Tequila** (blanco - Espolón or Cimarron)
6. **Sweet Vermouth** (Cocchi or Dolin)
7. **Dry Vermouth** (Dolin or Noilly Prat)
8. **Orange Liqueur** (Cointreau)

Plus: Angostura bitters, simple syrup, fresh citrus.

### The 15-Bottle Bar

Add these for broader range:

9. **Rye Whiskey** (Rittenhouse)
10. **Aged Rum** (Appleton Estate or El Dorado)
11. **Cognac** (Pierre Ferrand 1840)
12. **Campari**
13. **Maraschino Liqueur** (Luxardo)
14. **Elderflower Liqueur** (St-Germain)
15. **Mezcal** (Del Maguey Vida)

Plus: Orange bitters, honey, orgeat, Angostura bitters.

### The Deep Bar

Add as interests develop:

- Green and Yellow Chartreuse
- Aperol
- Amaro Nonino
- Cynar
- Bénédictine
- Peychaud's bitters
- Allspice dram
- Falernum
- Absinthe
- Various sherries
- Jamaican rum (Smith & Cross)
- Overproof rum

### Storage Notes

- **Spirits**: Room temperature, away from light
- **Vermouth**: Refrigerate after opening, use within 3-4 weeks
- **Liqueurs**: Most stable; cream-based need refrigeration
- **Citrus**: Room temperature short-term; refrigerate for longer storage
- **Syrups**: Refrigerate, use within 2-4 weeks (add vodka to extend)
