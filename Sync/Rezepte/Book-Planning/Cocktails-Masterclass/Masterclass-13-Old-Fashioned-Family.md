# Chapter 13: The Old Fashioned Family

The original cocktail template. Spirit, sugar, bitters, water. Everything else in cocktail history is variation on this theme.

When we say "cocktail," we're using a word that originally meant exactly this: spirit made palatable with sugar and aromatic bitters, diluted with water. The Old Fashioned preserves that definition in liquid form.

## The Template

**The Old Fashioned Formula:**
- 2 oz spirit (traditionally whiskey)
- 1/4 oz sweetener (sugar, syrup, or muddled cube)
- 2-3 dashes aromatic bitters
- Water (from ice dilution)

Build in glass, stir over ice, garnish with citrus.

### Why This Works

**Spirit** provides the foundation—the drink showcases one spirit with minimal modification.

**Sugar** rounds harsh edges, adds body, and makes higher-proof spirits more approachable.

**Bitters** add aromatic complexity, bridging spirit and sweetness.

**Water** (from ice dilution) opens flavors, integrates components, and reduces heat.

The proportions are minimal. This is a spirit-forward drink where you taste the whiskey (or other base) first, with sugar and bitters in supporting roles.

## The Master Recipe: Whiskey Old Fashioned

**Old Fashioned:**
- 2 oz bourbon or rye
- 1/4 oz simple syrup (or 1 sugar cube)
- 2 dashes Angostura bitters
- Orange twist for garnish

**Method 1 (Muddled Sugar):**
Place sugar cube in rocks glass. Add bitters and a splash of water. Muddle to dissolve. Add whiskey and ice. Stir until chilled. Garnish with expressed orange twist.

**Method 2 (Simple Syrup):**
Add whiskey, simple syrup, and bitters to mixing glass. Stir with ice until chilled. Strain over large ice cube in rocks glass. Garnish with expressed orange twist.

### Technique Notes

**Sugar cube vs. syrup:** Sugar cube is traditional and ritualistic. Syrup is faster and more consistent. Both produce excellent drinks.

**The ice:** One large cube is ideal—slow dilution maintains drink integrity. Standard cubes work but require adjustment (drink faster or accept more dilution).

**The muddling:** When using sugar cube, muddle until dissolved. Don't muddle fruit—the traditional Old Fashioned has no fruit in it, just an expressed twist.

**The twist:** Express orange oils over the surface. Some drop it in; some discard after expressing.

## The Fruit Controversy

Many bars serve "Old Fashioneds" with muddled orange and cherry. This is a different drink—not wrong, but not traditional.

**The traditional Old Fashioned:** No muddled fruit. Just spirit, sugar, bitters, twist.

**The Wisconsin/Midwestern Old Fashioned:** Muddled cherry and orange, sometimes with brandy, often with Sprite or soda. A regional variation, not the original.

**The compromise:** Garnish with orange twist and cherry but don't muddle them into the drink.

Know what you're making and why.

## The Variations

---

### Sazerac

The New Orleans classic—rye, absinthe rinse, Peychaud's.

- Absinthe (to rinse glass)
- 2 oz rye whiskey
- 1/4 oz simple syrup (or 1 sugar cube)
- 3 dashes Peychaud's bitters
- Lemon twist for garnish

Rinse chilled glass with absinthe, discarding excess. Build drink in separate glass with ice, stir, strain into prepared glass. Express lemon twist and discard.

**Character:** Anise undertones from absinthe, floral notes from Peychaud's. The New Orleans cocktail.

---

### Oaxaca Old Fashioned

Mezcal joins tequila in an agave split-base.

- 1.5 oz reposado tequila
- 0.5 oz mezcal
- 1 barspoon agave syrup
- 2 dashes Angostura bitters
- Orange twist for garnish

Build in glass over large ice. Stir. Flame orange twist over drink.

**Character:** Smoky, earthy, the mezcal adding complexity without overwhelming. Phil Ward created this at Death & Co.

---

### Rum Old Fashioned

Aged rum as the base.

- 2 oz aged rum
- 1/4 oz demerara syrup
- 2 dashes Angostura bitters
- 1 dash orange bitters
- Orange twist for garnish

Build over large ice. Stir.

**Character:** Tropical warmth, caramel notes from rum and demerara.

---

### Brandy Old Fashioned

The Wisconsin standard.

- 2 oz brandy
- 1 sugar cube
- 2 dashes Angostura bitters
- Orange slice and cherry (muddled)
- Lemon-lime soda or water

Muddle sugar, bitters, orange, and cherry. Add brandy and ice. Top with soda (or not). Stir.

**Character:** Sweeter, fruitier, a regional tradition. Not the "original" but beloved in its territory.

---

### Improved Whiskey Cocktail

The Old Fashioned's fancy cousin—adding maraschino and absinthe.

- 2 oz bourbon or rye
- 1/4 oz simple syrup
- 1/4 oz maraschino liqueur
- 2 dashes Angostura bitters
- 1 dash absinthe
- Lemon twist for garnish

Stir with ice. Strain over large ice cube.

**Character:** More complex, the liqueurs adding layers without overwhelming. A 19th-century evolution.

---

### Toronto

Canadian whisky with Fernet-Branca.

- 2 oz Canadian whisky
- 1/4 oz Fernet-Branca
- 1/4 oz simple syrup
- 2 dashes Angostura bitters
- Orange twist for garnish

Stir and strain.

**Character:** Bitter, herbal, the Fernet adding significant complexity.

---

### Benton's Old Fashioned

Bacon fat-washed bourbon.

- 2 oz bacon fat-washed bourbon
- 1/4 oz grade B maple syrup
- 2 dashes Angostura bitters
- Orange twist for garnish

Build over large ice. Stir.

**Character:** Savory, smoky, breakfast-adjacent. Don Lee created this at PDT, pioneering fat-washing.

## The Science

### Dilution and Spirit Character

The Old Fashioned's dilution serves a specific purpose: opening the spirit.

Undiluted high-proof whiskey can be overwhelming—alcohol heat masks flavor. As water enters the system:
- Alcohol molecules disperse
- Aromatic compounds release
- Flavor becomes perceivable

Target dilution: 15-20%. Less and the drink is hot; more and it's watery.

### Bitters Chemistry

Angostura bitters contain dozens of botanical extracts—cinnamon, clove, allspice, and more. These compounds:
- Share aromatic families with barrel-aged spirits
- Bridge sugar and spirit flavors
- Add complexity beyond their volume suggests

Two dashes is approximately 1/16 oz. A tiny amount with outsized impact.

### Sugar's Role

Sugar doesn't just add sweetness. It:
- Increases viscosity (body, mouthfeel)
- Reduces perceived harshness
- Extends finish
- Helps aromatics linger

This is why an Old Fashioned without sugar tastes incomplete, not just less sweet.

## Common Mistakes and Fixes

### Too Sweet

**Cause:** Over-sugaring, especially with cube method (cube plus syrup, or too much muddling liquid).

**Fix:** Reduce sweetener. One cube OR 1/4 oz syrup, not both.

### Fruit Overload

**Cause:** Overzealous muddling of fruit.

**Fix:** Don't muddle fruit. Garnish only, or use traditional no-fruit preparation.

### Under-Stirred

**Cause:** Insufficient dilution.

**Fix:** Stir longer with ice. The drink should feel integrated, not hot.

### Bitters Overkill

**Cause:** Heavy-handed dashing.

**Fix:** Two dashes is sufficient. Add more only if deliberately seeking intense bitters character.

## Choosing Your Components

### Whiskey

**High-rye bourbon** (Four Roses, Bulleit): Spicy, stands up to sugar and bitters.

**Standard bourbon** (Buffalo Trace, Maker's Mark): Sweeter, softer.

**Rye** (Rittenhouse, Old Overholt): Traditional, spicy, classic.

**Higher proof** (Booker's, Knob Creek): Can handle more dilution, more intense.

### Sweetener

**Simple syrup**: Clean, consistent, fast.

**Rich syrup (2:1)**: More body, less dilution needed.

**Demerara syrup**: Deeper, more complex sweetness.

**Maple syrup**: Specific flavor, works with certain whiskeys.

**Sugar cube**: Traditional, ritualistic, requires proper muddling.

### Bitters

**Angostura**: The default. Baking spices, depth.

**Orange bitters**: Citrus note, can combine with Angostura.

**Peychaud's**: For Sazerac specifically, floral profile.

**Chocolate bitters**: Adds cocoa depth.

**Specialty bitters**: Endless options for variation.

## The Ritual

The Old Fashioned invites ritual. The muddling of sugar. The careful dashing of bitters. The deliberate stirring. The expressed twist.

This isn't pretension—it's participation. Making an Old Fashioned properly connects you to 200 years of cocktail history. The ritual matters.

Embrace it. Don't rush. The drink improves with attention.
