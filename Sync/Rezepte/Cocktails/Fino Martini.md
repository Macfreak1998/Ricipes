# Zutaten
- dashes [[Fino Sherry]]
- 1 1/2 [[Gin]]
- [[Lemon]] peel

# Zubereitung
[[Stir]]: Fino Sherry, Gin
[[Strain]]: Martini Glass (chilled)
Twist lemon peel over drink and drop into glass

# Served
* Glass: [[Martini Glass]]
* Ice: [[Ice Cubes]]
* Garnish: [[Lemon]] peel

# History
The "Fino Martini" is a sophisticated variation of the classic Martini cocktail, where Fino Sherry is used in place of dry vermouth. This substitution imparts a distinct salinity, acidity, and nuttiness to the drink, differentiating it from the herbal and bitter notes of traditional vermouth. While it's likely that such a drink was made before, a notable appearance of the Fino Martini recipe is in Stan Jones' *Complete Barguide* from 1977.

The history of the **Martini cocktail** is somewhat shrouded in mystery, with several theories regarding its origin. The move towards a "dry" Martini began to appear in cocktail books around 1903.

**Fino Sherry** is the driest and palest of the traditional varieties of sherry, a fortified wine produced in Andalusia, Spain. Its defining characteristic is its biological aging under a layer of "flor" yeast, which protects the wine from oxidation and imparts unique flavors, including a salty note. The "Fino Martini" combines the historical elegance of the Martini with the unique characteristics of Fino Sherry, creating a crisp, dry, and subtly complex aperitif.
