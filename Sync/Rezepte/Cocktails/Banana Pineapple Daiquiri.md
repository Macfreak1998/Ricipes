# Zutaten

-   1 oz [[White Rum]]
-   2 oz [[Pineapple Juice]]
-   1 [[Banana]] (sliced)
-   1 tsp [[Simple Syrup]]
-   1 cup [[Crushed Ice]]

# Zubereitung
Combine Rum, Pineapple Juice, Sliced Banana, Sugar, Crushed Ice in a blender
[[Blend]] until smooth
Pour into chilled glass

# Served
*   Glass: [[Cocktail Glass]]
*   Ice: [[Crushed Ice]]
*   Garnish: [[Pineapple Slice]] or [[Banana Slice]]

# History
The "Banana Pineapple Daiquiri" is a tropical twist on the classic Daiquiri, a cocktail with a rich history rooted in Cuba. The original Daiquiri was invented in 1898 in Cuba by American mining engineer Jennings Cox, a simple concoction of rum, lime juice, and sugar. Over time, the Daiquiri evolved, with Constantino Ribalaigua Vert, a renowned bartender at El Floridita in Havana, credited with popularizing the use of chipped ice and electric blenders to create frozen variations.

The Banana Daiquiri, a popular variation, emerged around the 1970s at St. Thomas' Mountaintop bar in the U.S. Virgin Islands. The Banana Pineapple Daiquiri further builds on this fruity evolution, combining the distinct tropical flavors of both banana and pineapple. While a specific origin for the exact combination of banana and pineapple in a Daiquiri isn't widely documented, it aligns with the broader trend of incorporating various tropical fruits into the classic rum-based cocktail. The pineapple itself is often seen as a symbol of hospitality, friendship, and warm weather, adding to the drink's inviting appeal.

The Banana Pineapple Daiquiri, like many tropical fruit-infused Daiquiris, evokes a sense of relaxation, vacation, and exotic locales. It's a drink often associated with warm weather and a desire for a taste of the tropics.
