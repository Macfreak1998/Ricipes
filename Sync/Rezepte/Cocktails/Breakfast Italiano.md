# Zutaten

- 1.5 [[Limoncello]]
- 0.5 [[Vodka]]
- 1 [[Espresso]]
- 0.25 [[Simple Syrup]]

# Zubereitung
[[Shake]]: Limoncello, Vodka, Espresso, Simple Syrup with ice until well-chilled and frothy
[[Strain]]

# Served
* Glass: [[Coupe]] or [[Nick and Nora Glass]]
* Ice: [[Straight]]
* Garnish: Lemon twist

# History
The Breakfast Italiano is a modern craft cocktail featured by @theeducatedbarfly with the note: "I've been known to punk the Espresso Martini from time to time.... AND IT IS DESERVED."

**The Espresso Martini Backlash:**

The Educated Barfly's "punk the Espresso Martini" comment references a real tension in cocktail culture. The Espresso Martini, despite being delicious and popular, has become somewhat controversial among craft bartenders because:
- **Overordered**: Often the only cocktail many guests know
- **Time-consuming**: Requires proper shaking for foam
- **Equipment issues**: Can damage certain shaker seals
- **Basic perception**: Seen as an entry-level cocktail

However, DiMonriva's "AND IT IS DESERVED" (in caps) suggests the Espresso Martini's ubiquity is earned—it's popular because it's genuinely good.

**The Italian Twist:**

The Breakfast Italiano reimagines the Espresso Martini through an Italian lens:

**Classic Espresso Martini:**
- Vodka
- Coffee liqueur (Kahlúa)
- Fresh espresso
- Simple syrup

**Breakfast Italiano:**
- **Limoncello** (Italian lemon liqueur)
- **Vodka** (reduced amount)
- **Espresso** (Italian coffee)
- **Simple Syrup** (minimal)

**Why Limoncello Works:**

Limoncello + espresso might sound strange, but it's actually brilliant:
- **Lemon and coffee**: Classic Italian pairing (espresso with lemon peel is traditional)
- **Sweetness**: Limoncello provides both lemon flavor and sweetness
- **Lower coffee liqueur**: No Kahlúa means the espresso flavor is cleaner, brighter
- **Breakfast vibes**: Lemon and coffee evoke Italian breakfast culture

**The "Breakfast" Name:**

"Breakfast Italiano" cleverly positions this as a morning drink, which makes sense in Italian culture:
- **Caffè e cornetto**: Classic Italian breakfast is espresso and pastry
- **Limoncello**: Often enjoyed after dinner but also morning-appropriate in its brightness
- **Pick-me-up**: Coffee cocktails work as brunch drinks or afternoon revivals

**Italian Coffee Culture:**

This cocktail pays homage to Italian caffè culture:
- **Espresso quality matters**: Italians are serious about coffee
- **Lemon tradition**: Many Italians request a lemon twist with espresso
- **Simple ingredients**: Italian cocktails often feature fewer, higher-quality ingredients

**For Coffee Snobs:**

The Breakfast Italiano appeals to coffee enthusiasts who find Kahlúa too sweet or artificial. By removing coffee liqueur and letting real espresso shine, this drink showcases actual coffee flavor rather than masking it.

**The "Punking" Philosophy:**

Creating variations on overplayed cocktails isn't disrespectful—it's evolutionary. The Breakfast Italiano proves the Espresso Martini template (spirit + espresso + sweetener) is flexible enough for creative interpretation. If you can "punk" a classic and make something better (or at least equally good), you've proven the original's strength, not its weakness.

**Modern Brunch Culture:**

The Breakfast Italiano fits perfectly into contemporary brunch drinking culture where:
- Coffee cocktails are increasingly popular
- Italian ingredients signal sophistication
- Lighter, brighter drinks work better than heavy classics
- Instagram-worthy foam tops remain essential

**The Foam Factor:**

Like all espresso cocktails, proper shaking is crucial—the vigorous shake creates the signature foam cap that makes these drinks visually appealing and texturally interesting. The lemon oils from the twist combine beautifully with this foam.

The Breakfast Italiano represents modern bartending's confidence to reinvent even the most popular cocktails, proving that the best way to honor a classic isn't blind replication but thoughtful evolution that respects the original while creating something distinctly new.
