# Zutaten
- 1.5 oz [[Aged Rum]]
- 0.75 oz [[White Rum]]
- 1 oz [[Passionfruit Puree]] (Fresh or good quality juice)
- 0.75 oz [[Lime Juice]] (Fresh)
- 0.5 oz [[Orgeat]]
- 0.25 oz [[Allspice Dram]]
- Dash of [[Angostura Bitters]]

# Zubereitung
1. Combine all ingredients (Aged Rum, White Rum, Passionfruit Puree, Lime Juice, Orgeat, Allspice Dram, Bitters) in a cocktail shaker.
2. Fill the shaker with ice and [[Shake]] vigorously until well-chilled (about 20 seconds).
3. [[Strain]] into a chilled [[Tiki Mug]] or a large [[Highball Glass]] filled with [[Crushed Ice]].

# Served
* Glass: [[Tiki Mug]] or [[Highball Glass]]
* Ice: [[Crushed Ice]]
* Garnish: [[Mint Sprig]], [[Edible Flower]], or [[Passionfruit Half]]

# History
The "Passionfruit Paradise Punch" is a modern tropical punch that draws upon the long tradition of punch-making and the widespread use of passion fruit in beverages. While there isn't a single, documented historical origin for this specific named cocktail, its identity is a combination of the rich histories of its two main components: passion fruit and punch.

**Origin and Meaning of "Punch":**
The concept of "punch" as a mixed drink dates back to 17th-century India. The word "punch" is believed to be derived from the Hindi word "paanch," meaning "five," referring to the traditional five main ingredients: alcohol, sugar, lemon or lime, water, and spices. English sailors and merchants of the British East India Company encountered the drink during their voyages, adapting local ingredients to create a refreshing beverage. Punch gained popularity in Europe, particularly in England, where it became a convivial drink. West Indian rum eventually became the primary spirit, and the addition of orange juice to punch recipes became popular in the 18th century.

**Origin and Meaning of "Passion Fruit":**
Passion fruit, scientifically known as *Passiflora Edulis*, is native to the Amazon rainforests of Brazil. The name "passion fruit" has a religious connotation; Spanish missionaries in South America saw the flower of the passion fruit vine as a symbol of the Crucifixion of Christ. In the 16th century, Portuguese and Spanish explorers introduced passion fruit seeds to their respective countries, leading to its spread across Asia, Africa, and Oceania. Today, it is widely used in juices, cocktails, desserts, and other culinary applications, adding a tropical vibrancy.

This "Passionfruit Paradise Punch" recipe features Aged Rum, White Rum, Passionfruit Puree, Lime Juice, Orgeat, Allspice Dram, and Angostura Bitters. It is a complex and flavorful rum-based punch, combining the rich and tropical notes of various rums and passion fruit with the tartness of lime, the sweetness of orgeat, and the aromatic spice of allspice dram and Angostura bitters. The "Paradise" in its name likely evokes the tropical and refreshing qualities associated with passion fruit and punch, creating an indulgent and exotic experience.
