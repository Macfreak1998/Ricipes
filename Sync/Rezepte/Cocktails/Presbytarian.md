# History
The "Presbytarian" cocktail is almost certainly a misspelling of the "Presbyterian" cocktail, a classic highball believed to have originated in the late 1890s.

The drink is typically made with whiskey (often Scotch or rye), ginger ale, and sometimes a splash of club soda. The exact origin of its name is not definitively known, but it has been associated with the drink for over a century. One theory suggests the name might come from "presb√Ωteros," the Greek word for 'elder,' linking it to the Presbyterian Church. Another less substantiated explanation posits that the name is younger than the drink itself, stemming from a mocktail of ginger ale and soda served to teetotaling Presbyterians at 1950s cocktail parties.

By 1900, a variation of the Presbyterian emerged with the addition of lime, becoming known as the Mamie Taylor. This addition of lime juice made the Mamie Taylor a very popular and more balanced drink. The Presbyterian remains a simple, refreshing, and enduring classic.
