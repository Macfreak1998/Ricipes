# Zutaten
- 1 [[Brandy]]
- 1/4 [[Amaretto]]
- 1 cup hot [[Espresso]]
- brown [[Simple Syrup]]
- [[Heavy Cream]]

# Zubereitung
Heat brandy and amaretto in Irish coffee glass (do not boil)
Add sugar
Fill with hot coffee
Stir
Top with cream
(Galliano may be substituted for amaretto)

# Served
* Glass: [[Irish Coffee Glass]]
* Ice:
* Garnish: cream

# History
The "Italian Coffee" cocktail, as presented in this recipe, is a warm, coffee-based drink featuring Brandy, Amaretto, hot Espresso, Sugar, and Heavy Cream. While there isn't a single, widely recognized cocktail specifically named "Italian Coffee" with a distinct historical origin, it is a modern creation designed to evoke Italian coffee culture and the tradition of combining coffee with spirits.

Coffee was introduced to Italy in the 16th century through Venice, and by the 18th century, coffee houses flourished. The invention of the espresso machine in the late 19th century (patented by Angelo Moriondo in 1884) solidified espresso as the foundation of Italian coffee culture.
