# Zutaten
- 1.75 oz [[Gin]]
- 0.50 oz [[Cointreau]] or [[Orange Liqueur]]
- 0.50 oz [[Lemon Juice]]
- 0.50 oz [[Cucumber Simple Syrup]]
- 2 heaping bar-spoonfuls [[Greek Yogurt]]

# Zubereitung
1. Add all cocktail ingredients into a cocktail shaker with ice.
2. [[Shake]] and [[Double Strain]] with a [[Hawthorne Strainer]] into a [[Cocktail Coupe]].
3. Garnish as desired, for example, with a [[Cucumber Slice]] and [[Mint Sprig]].

## Cucumber Simple Syrup
### Zutaten
* [[Cucumber]]
* [[Sugar]]
* [[Water]]

### Zubereitung
1. Combine Cucumbers, Sugar, and Water in a small saucepaccordingly.
2. Bring to a boil, then remove from heat.
3. Let cool for 20 minutes and [[Strain]] out the Cucumbers.
4. Store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks in an airtight container.

# Served
* Glass: [[Cocktail Coupe]]
* Ice: [[None]] (served up)
* Garnish: [[Cucumber Slice]], [[Mint Sprig]]

# History
The "Cucumber Greek Yogurt Cocktail" is a modern, creative cocktail that does not have a widely documented historical origin. It is likely a contemporary creation, emerging from the trend of incorporating fresh, savory, and sometimes unexpected ingredients into mixology.

This cocktail is clearly inspired by the flavors of Tzatziki, a traditional Greek yogurt and cucumber dish. Tzatziki itself has a rich history, with the combination of yogurt and cucumber believed to have originated around 1469 CE, used by ancient Greeks for its refreshing and healthy qualities.
