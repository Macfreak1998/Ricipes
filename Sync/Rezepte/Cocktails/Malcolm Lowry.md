# History
The "Malcolm Lowry cocktail" was created by drinks author David Broom. It is named after Malcolm Lowry's 1947 novel *Under the Volcano*, which delves into a man's struggle with alcoholism in Mexico. Malcolm Lowry, an English poet and novelist, is best known for *Under the Volcano*. His personal life was significantly impacted by alcoholism, a theme prominently featured in his work. He spent time in Mexico, where he continued to drink heavily and developed his seminal novel.

The cocktail itself is often described as a blend of a Daiquiri and a Margarita, characterized by its "funky" and "punchy" flavor. The inclusion of both rum and tequila, along with citrus and triple sec, reflects the diverse and potent influences that might have inspired a drink named after such a literary figure.
