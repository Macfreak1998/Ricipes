# Heavy Stir

## Ingredients
- 2 oz (60ml) Spirit (e.g., Gin, Whiskey, Rum)
- 1 oz (30ml) Sweet Vermouth
- 2 dashes Aromatic Bitters
- Orange peel for garnish

## Instructions
1. Combine the spirit, sweet vermouth, and bitters in a mixing glass.
2. Fill the mixing glass with ice.
3. Stir vigorously for 30-60 seconds until well-chilled and adequately diluted.
4. Strain into a chilled coupe or old-fashioned glass over fresh ice (or neat).
5. Express the oils from an orange peel over the drink and drop it in as a garnish.

# History
The term "Heavy Stir" in cocktail nomenclature refers to the technique of thoroughly stirring a cocktail, particularly those that are spirit-forward. This method is crucial for achieving a specific texture, clarity, and dilution in drinks made solely with spirits, liqueurs, and vermouths, without introducing air bubbles that would make them cloudy or frothy.

The practice of stirring cocktails dates back to the early days of mixed drinks, with cocktail manuals like Jerry Thomas's 1862 *Bar-Tender's Guide* specifying stirring for many concoctions. Over time, a clearer understanding emerged regarding when to stir versus shake a cocktail, with stirring being preferred for drinks where clarity and a smooth, silky texture are desired.

The primary purpose of a "heavy stir" is to gently combine ingredients, chill the drink, and achieve a precise level of dilution. This slower process allows for greater control over the dilution, which is crucial for spirit-forward drinks where over-dilution can mask the nuanced flavors of the spirits. It also ensures that the individual components retain some of their distinctiveness, creating a layered flavor profile where the spirits remain the "star of the show." This "Heavy Stir" cocktail is a generic representation of such a drink, emphasizing the technique over a specific historical recipe.
