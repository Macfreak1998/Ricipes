# Zutaten
- 1 oz [[Gold Rum]]
- 0.5 oz [[Dark Rum]]
- 0.5 oz [[Jamaican Rum]]
- 0.25 oz [[Creme de Cacao White]]
- 0.5 oz [[Simple Syrup]]
- 2 dashes [[Orinoco Bitters]]
- 1 Dash [[Bittermans Mole Bitters]]

# Zubereitung
1. Combine all ingredients in a mixing glass with ice.
2. [[Stir]] until well-chilled.
3. [[Strain]] into a [[Rocks Glass]] over a large ice cube.

# Served
* Glass: [[Rocks Glass]]
* Ice: [[Large Ice Cube]]
* Garnish:

# History
"The Dockside: A Killer Rum Old Fashioned Cocktail" is a modern creation, not a historical cocktail with a long lineage. Its development was inspired by two main factors: the creator's personal quest to visit top bars globally and the Dead Rabbit bar's effort to recreate the extinct "London dockside rum" style.

The broader "Old Fashioned" cocktail, from which "The Dockside" draws its inspiration, has a much longer history. It originated in the early 1800s as a "whiskey cocktail," defined by its simple components: spirits, bitters, water, and sugar. The name "Old Fashioned" came about in the 1870s and 1880s when patrons, preferring the original, less embellished version, would specifically ask for an "old-fashioned whiskey cocktail."

The Rum Old Fashioned, a variation that substitutes rum for whiskey, is believed to have originated in the Caribbean or Latin America, regions where rum is a prominent spirit.
