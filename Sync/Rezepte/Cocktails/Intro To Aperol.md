# History
The "Intro To Aperol" cocktail is a modern creation, likely designed to introduce drinkers to the unique flavor profile of Aperol, an iconic Italian bitter aperitif. While there isn't a specific historical origin or creator widely documented for a cocktail explicitly named "Intro To Aperol," its existence is a testament to Aperol's versatility and growing popularity in contemporary mixology.

Aperol was created in 1919 by brothers Luigi and Silvio Barbieri in Padua, Italy, making its debut at the Padova World Fair. Aperol is distinguished by its bright orange hue and a bittersweet orange flavor, with a recipe that includes botanical ingredients such as gentian, rhubarb, and cinchona. While it gained some traction after World War II, the Aperol Spritz cocktail, made with Aperol, Prosecco, and soda water, rose to prominence in the 1950s. Today, Aperol is widely recognized as a symbol of Italian social culture and the "dolce vita" lifestyle.
