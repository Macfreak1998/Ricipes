# History
The "Periodista cocktail" is a drink with Cuban origins, and its name means "journalist" in Spanish.

Its history traces back to Havana, Cuba, with the earliest known recipe appearing in the 1948 "El Arte de Cantinera," a bartenders' manual from the Club de Cantineros de la Republica de Cuba. Legend suggests it was a favorite of Ernest Hemingway at El Floridita in Havana. Another historical anecdote links its popularity to reporters in Cuban bars during the 1962 Cuban Missile Crisis.

The Periodista is often described as a variation of a Daiquiri, characterized by its bright and citrusy flavor profile, where tart lime and sweet apricot notes play a significant role.
