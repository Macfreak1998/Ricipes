# Pisco

## Beschreibung
Pisco is the national drink of Chile and rich in tradition. The base wine is mainly made from Muscat grapes and grows north of Santiago de Chile in precisely defined official regions. An aging period in oak or clay vessels is followed by the distillation process.

## Herstellung
The base wine for Pisco is mainly produced from Muscat grapes. These are grown in officially defined regions north of Santiago de Chile. After winemaking, an aging period in oak barrels or clay vessels follows before the distillation process is carried out.

## Flavor Profile
Pisco is typically clear, light, and somewhat fruity with Muscat notes.

## Known Brands
The most popular Pisco in Chile - with annual sales of 23 million bottles - is **Pisco Control** from La Serena.

Other well-known brands:
- Control Gran Pisco - Gran Pisco, Chile, 86° proof
- Pisco Capel - Chile, 86° proof

## Regionale Varianten
Other South American countries that produce brandy are Argentina, Bolivia, and Peru. Peru also produces Pisco, which differs from the Chilean style.

## Verwendung in Cocktails
**Pisco Sour** is the most famous Pisco bar drink and one of the classic South American cocktails.
