# Calvados

## Beschreibung

Calvados is a French apple brandy from Normandy, a region in northwestern France that is world-famous for its apples and apple production. Unlike Cognac or Armagnac, which are distilled from grapes, Calvados is made from apple cider (Cidre) - sometimes with a small proportion of pears (Poir√©). The result is a spirit with distinctive apple aromatics, ranging from young and fruity to complex and aged.

Production begins with the fermentation of apple cider, which is then distilled in pot stills or column stills. The distillation method strongly influences the final profile: pot still Calvados (traditional) is fruitier and more complex, while column still Calvados (modern) is lighter and cleaner. After distillation, Calvados ages in oak barrels - often former Cognac or sherry casks - where it develops color, tannins, and additional complexity. The alcohol content is typically 40% ABV.

The taste profile varies depending on age and production method. Young Calvados (2-3 years) shows fresh, intense apple flavors with a certain sharpness. Older Calvados (8+ years) develops notes of dried apples, vanilla, caramel, spices, and oak, while the apple base remains noticeable. The best Calvados has a beautiful balance between fruitiness, wood influence, and complexity.

There are various quality grades: Calvados AOC (general appellation), Calvados Pays d'Auge AOC (strict regulations, pot still distillation), and Calvados Domfrontais AOC (with pears). As with Cognac, there are age statements: VS (at least 2 years), VSOP (at least 4 years), XO or Hors d'Age (at least 6 years).

## Verwendung in Cocktails

In cocktails, Calvados offers a fascinating alternative to other brandies. It works excellently in autumn and winter drinks, where its apple notes fit perfectly. Classic cocktails such as the Jack Rose (Calvados, Grenadine, Lime), Corpse Reviver No. 1 (Calvados, Cognac, Sweet Vermouth), or Widow's Kiss (Calvados, Yellow Chartreuse, Benedictine, Bitters) demonstrate its versatility.

Calvados can also be used in Sours, Old Fashioneds, or as a modifier in spirit-forward drinks. Its apple aromatics harmonize well with citrus, honey, spices, and other brandies. Modern bartenders often use it to impart an autumnal or "orchard fruit" note to drinks. A good VSOP Calvados is a smart investment for the home bar, especially if you want to go beyond the standard spirits.
